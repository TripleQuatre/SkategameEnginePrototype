Main uncovered or weakly covered interaction zones

1. V10 rules under persistence stress
- `Multiple Attack` + save/load
- `No Repetition` + save/load
- `Switch normal` + save/load
- `Switch verified` + save/load
- `Order/Relevance` after save/load in both duel and battle

2. V10 rules under deep undo stress
- `Switch normal` after several undo levels
- `Switch verified` after several undo levels
- `Attack/Repetition` synergy after undo from setup or engaged turns
- `Order/Relevance` after undoing setup-side changes and restarting

3. V10 rule interactions not yet isolated enough
- `Multiple Attack` + `No Repetition`
- `Multiple Attack` + `Switch`
- `Switch` + `Uniqueness`
- `Switch` + `Repetition`
- `Switch verified` + attack-failure recovery
- `Order/Relevance` + battle setups

4. Battle-specific V10 coverage is still weaker than duel coverage
- `Order = relevance` in battle
- `Multiple Attack` in battle
- `Switch` in battle
- mixed V10 rules + roster transitions in battle

5. Harness matrix coverage is still mostly V8/V9.3 centered
- current matrix does not yet use explicit V10-oriented presets
- current matrix does not yet explore V10-specific combinations systematically

Still needing broader V10 crosses
- `new game` under V10-heavy setups
- `history` with V10-specific attack traces (`V N(V)`, `V N(X)`) in longer sequences
- `setup details` after persistence and undo in V10 setups
- roster transitions after V10 profile/order setup choices

Audit conclusion
The harness is already strong enough to protect V10 baseline behavior, but it is not yet
wide enough to claim robust coverage of the full V10 interaction space.

Current confidence level
- high on primary flows
- moderate on rule-by-rule correctness
- moderate-to-low on rare multi-rule + persistence + undo combinations

V10.1 should therefore focus on
- more contrastive fixed presets
- V10-oriented stress scenarios
- broader `preset x scenario` crossings
- dedicated regressions for V10 subtleties
- stronger battle coverage for V10 rules
